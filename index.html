What it does

Set it once. Forget it's there.

Everything you'd want from an unattended screen recorder — and nothing you wouldn't.

On your schedule

Every few seconds or minutes — your call — with a live countdown to the next shot. Capture all displays, one screen, or a single stitched panorama.

Delivered to your folder

Frames land in a private folder on your Mac first, then copy to the destination you pick — an smb:// NAS share or any local folder.

Never loses a frame

If the destination goes offline, captures queue safely on your Mac and upload automatically when it returns — with smart retry and backoff.

Private by design

Auto-pause when a password manager or bank app is in front. A system-wide pause hotkey. Nothing leaves your Mac except to the folder you chose.

Light on space

HEIC by default for tiny, crisp files (JPEG and PNG too), an optional resolution cap, and automatic cleanup once uploaded — or after a set number of days.

Effortless & up to date

Lives in the menu bar with one gentle pulse while it works. Launches at login, and updates itself automatically — no fuss, ever.

Getting started

Up and running in a minute.

No account, no fuss — install, allow, point it at a folder.

  1. 1

    Download & install

    Open the installer above and click Continue → Install. Screenlapse lands in your Applications folder and opens right away — no security warnings to click through.

    Install Screenlapse
    Screenlapse will be installed in your Applications folder.
    Install
  2. 2

    Allow Screen Recording

    On first launch, Screenlapse's welcome window asks for Screen Recording. Click Allow, then Open System Settings, and switch Screenlapse on under Privacy & Security → Screen Recording. Quit and reopen Screenlapse once so macOS applies it.

    Screen Recording
    “Screenlapse” would like to record this screen.
    Open System Settings
    Deny
    Screenlapse
  3. 3

    Allow direct capture

    When macOS asks Screenlapse to “bypass the private window picker”, click Allow. That lets Screenlapse capture your whole screen on schedule, without you choosing a window each time. It's expected — and only Screenlapse is doing it.

    “Screenlapse” wants to bypass the window picker
    …to directly access your screen.
    Allow
    Open System Settings
  4. 4

    Choose where captures go

    Open Settings → Destination and point Screenlapse at your folder — a NAS share like smb://nas.local/Screenshots, or any local folder. Done: it now captures on your schedule and delivers every frame, quietly from the menu bar.

    Where captures go
    smb://nas.local/Screenshots
    ✓ Reachable · up to date

Yours, and only yours.

Screenlapse explains before it ever asks for permission, captures only the screen — never your microphone or camera, and pauses — never saving black frames — if you turn Screen Recording access off. It's a native menu-bar app: no account, no cloud, no telemetry. Nothing leaves your Mac except to the folder you chose.
